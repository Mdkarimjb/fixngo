## 1. Definitions

- **"Proposal"** means the document titled *"Proposal for Development of a Unified Web & Mobile Application – FixNGo"* dated 07 July 2026, attached as **Annexure A**, which sets out the scope, technology stack, timeline, milestones, and commercials.
- **"Application"** / **"Deliverables"** means the unified Progressive Web App (Web + installable Android/iOS), backend API, and associated documentation described in the Proposal.
- **"Milestone"** means a stage of work as listed in Section 9 of the Proposal.
- **"Go-Live"** means the date the Application is deployed to production and made available to the Client.
- **"Confidential Information"** means any non-public business, technical, or financial information disclosed by one Party to the other.

## 2. Scope of Work

2.1 The Developer will design, develop, and deliver the Application strictly in accordance with the scope defined in the Proposal (Annexure A).

2.2 Any work, feature, integration, or change **not expressly listed** in the Proposal is out of scope and will be handled under Section 8 (Change Requests).

## 3. Term

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until the Application is delivered, accepted, and fully paid for, unless terminated earlier under Section 12.

## 4. Fees & Payment Terms

4.1 The total development fee is **INR 4,50,000 (one-time)**, payable in milestone instalments as set out in Section 9 of the Proposal.

4.2 Each milestone invoice is payable within **five (5) business days** of being raised.

4.3 Deliverables for a milestone are released **only after** that milestone payment is received in full.

4.4 Hosting, app-store subscriptions, third-party service charges, and AMC are billed separately as per the Proposal, at actuals.

4.5 Applicable taxes (GST) are charged additionally as per prevailing rates.

4.6 **Late payment:** Any invoice unpaid beyond the due date may attract interest of **[1.5]% per month** on the outstanding amount, and entitles the Developer to suspend work under Section 5.

## 5. Suspension for Non-Payment or Client Delay

5.1 If any payment is delayed beyond **[7] days** from the due date, **OR** the Client fails to respond to a request for feedback, approval, information, or third-party access within **[10] business days**, the Developer may **suspend all work** on written notice, without liability for resulting delays.

5.2 During suspension, timelines are paused and extended by the length of the delay.

## 6. Project Abandonment (Termination for Client Inaction)

6.1 If, after suspension under Section 5, the Client **remains in default of payment** or **remains unresponsive** for a further continuous period of **[15] days**, the project shall be treated as **abandoned by the Client**.

6.2 On abandonment:
   (a) this Agreement stands **terminated** without further notice;
   (b) all **amounts invoiced up to the date of abandonment remain due and payable**, and any advance/paid amounts are **non-refundable**;
   (c) work completed but not yet paid for is **not handed over**, and the Developer retains all rights to it until settled;
   (d) the Developer is released from all further obligations, including timelines and warranty;
   (e) the Developer may, at its sole discretion and without liability, **discontinue, archive, take offline, and permanently delete/discard** the work-in-progress, source code, builds, environments, and associated data, and is under **no obligation to preserve or restore** them. The Client waives any claim arising from such deletion.

6.3 If the Client wishes to restart an abandoned project, it may be treated as **new work**, re-quoted and re-scheduled at the Developer's discretion. Any work discarded under Clause 6.2(e) may have to be **rebuilt and re-charged**.

## 7. Client Responsibilities

The Client shall, in a timely manner: provide content, branding, and information required; grant access to required third-party accounts (payment gateway, MSG91/SMS, WhatsApp Business, maps, Play Store, Apple Developer, hosting); review deliverables and provide consolidated feedback; and nominate a single point of contact for approvals.

## 8. Change Requests

Any change outside the agreed scope will be documented, estimated, and separately quoted. Work on a change begins only after written approval and agreed payment terms.

## 9. Milestone Acceptance & Sign-off

9.1 On completion of a Milestone, the Developer will notify the Client for review.

9.2 The Client shall review and either accept or provide written, in-scope feedback within **[5] business days**.

9.3 If no response is received within that period, the Milestone is deemed **accepted**.

## 10. Intellectual Property & Handover

10.1 Until **100% of the fees** are received, all IP in the Application, source code, and deliverables remains with the **Developer**.

10.2 Upon receipt of **full and final payment**, the Developer assigns to the Client ownership of the custom source code and delivered Application.

10.3 **Handover deliverables** (on full payment) include:
   - Source code repository access
   - Production deployment
   - Admin credentials
   - Environment/configuration documentation
   - Basic operational handover notes

10.4 Third-party libraries, frameworks, and services remain under their respective licenses. Third-party account subscriptions and fees are borne by the Client.

## 11. Warranty & Support

11.1 A **6-month free warranty** (bug fixing for in-scope defects) applies from the Go-Live date.

11.2 The warranty **excludes** new features, design changes, additional functionality, third-party service failures (payment gateway, SMS, WhatsApp, maps), and issues caused by Client-side changes or misuse.

11.3 Post-warranty support is available under the monthly **AMC** as per the Proposal. Warranty and support are **void** during any period of payment default.

## 12. Termination

12.1 **For cause:** Either Party may terminate on **[15] days** written notice if the other commits a material breach and fails to cure it within the notice period.

12.2 **By Client (convenience):** The Client may terminate on written notice; in that case all work completed and invoiced up to the termination date is payable, and advances are non-refundable.

12.3 **Client inaction:** Termination on abandonment is governed by Section 6.

12.4 On any termination, Sections 6.2, 10, 13, 14, and 16 survive.

## 13. Confidentiality

Each Party shall keep the other's Confidential Information secret, use it only for this project, and not disclose it to third parties, during the term and for **[2] years** after.

## 14. Limitation of Liability

14.1 The Developer's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the **total fees actually paid** by the Client.

14.2 Neither Party is liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential losses (including loss of profit, data, or business).

## 15. Data Protection

Each Party shall comply with applicable Indian data protection laws, including the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023**. The Client is the data controller for end-user data collected through the Application.

## 16. Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for delay or failure caused by events beyond reasonable control (natural disaster, war, government action, internet/hosting/third-party outages, etc.).

## 17. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

17.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of **India**.

17.2 Disputes shall first be attempted to be resolved amicably; failing which, they shall be subject to **arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996**, seated at **[City]**, before a sole arbitrator, in English.

17.3 Courts at **[City]** shall have exclusive jurisdiction.

18.1 This Agreement together with **Annexure A (Proposal)** is the entire agreement between the Parties. In case of conflict, this Agreement prevails over the Proposal.

18.2 Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

18.3 Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's written consent.
